Background & Aims

Quantifying the ecological drivers of pathogen transmission remains a key challenge in integrated phylodynamic modeling. While high-resolution data on climate, land use, and vector distributions exist, they remain underutilized due to limited accessibility and integration tools.

We present PhyloCov, a web-based application built on Google Earth Engine (GEE), designed to host, explore, and extract time-varying ecological covariates relevant to pathogen transmission. The platform aims to support streamlined access to Earth observation datasets as well as relevant species distribution layers to enhance the application of transmission models.

Methods & Results

PhyloCov leverages GEE’s extensive data catalog and app deployment system to provide users with interactive, time-aware selection tools for covariates such as precipitation, temperature, land cover, vegetation indices, and vector suitability.

Feature extraction enables point-and-region sampling over customizable timeframes. While pathogen-specific covariate prioritization is under development, the current version enables users to export model-ready covariate information for incorporation into phylodynamic inference pipelines.

Implications

By centralizing ecological covariate data exploration, visualization, and extraction in an accessible interface, PhyloCov facilitates reproducible integration of environmental drivers into infectious disease modeling.

This tool will support researchers seeking to understand ecological influences on pathogen spread, particularly in low-resource settings where such data are often fragmented. Future updates will focus on pathogen-specific categorisation and API functionality for large-scale data extraction. PhyloCov serves as a platform to enhance the application of transmission models by making ecological covariate data readily accessible for integrated phylodynamic studies.
